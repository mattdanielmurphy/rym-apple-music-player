Slushwave

AKA: Phaserwave Dreamtone • 1,203 releases
Utilizes a distinct form of sample manipulation to create a “slushy” sound, usually sequenced in soft Ambient-based soundscapes.
Read more Slushwave is a subgenre of Vaporwave, which distinguishes itself by its strong reliance on heavy sample manipulation to deliver a hypnagogic, thick sound distinctive to the style. The genre specializes in creating softly looping Ambient-based soundscapes through repetitive sequencing of its sample material, emphasizing dreamy atmospheres along with a characteristic “slushy” manipulation of sound, which is typically achieved through the multi-layering utilization of effects such as reverbs, delays, flangers, filters and especially phasers (hence the less frequently used term phaserwave). The sample material itself is often sourced from, but not limited to, similar genre origins as vaporwave typically draws from, but with a heavier focus laid upon East Asian genres such as City Pop, C-Pop or J-Pop. Though slushwave primarily originated from purely sample-based compositions, in recent years artists have begun creating entirely original slushwave by sampling self-written material.

Thematically, slushwave mostly sets itself apart from most of the established aesthetics of vaporwave, such as consumerism, corporate clichés or 1980s and 1990s nostalgia, and instead predominantly tries to evoke ideas of love, longing, melancholy and dreams, which go along with the lush and ambient nature of the music. Releases are often presented with minimal artwork like untreated stills of old visual media, such as movies or commercials, and titled using non-Latin alphabets, further mystifying the listening experience.

While the concept of an ambient approach towards vaporwave had been circulating the scene since the genre's inception, namely in releases such as REDEFINING THE WORKPLACE or Transversal Worldwide Shopping, the term "slushwave" was only later coined by t e l e p a t h テレパシー能力者 in 2013. It was subsequently popularized in the following years, mainly through popular releases under t e l e p a t h's own aliases and collaborations, but also through the works of a handful of producers who adopted the genre very early on, such as MindSpring Memories, G a t e w a y ゲートウェイ, VAV-31 and HUMANIGHTMARE. These further evolved the genre's sound and helped establish slushwave into a solidified subgenre of vaporwave. In 2019 the genre would see a significant growth in popularity, as new directions and possibilities for the genre were brought up by desert sand feels warm at night's well-recieved introduction of a sample-free approach to its creation. This sparked a rise of new producers taking up the genre, as well as a general larger presence of the genre within online communities.

Often, slushwave is confused for Dreampunk, due to their similar ideas of ambient-focused soundscapes under the broad umbrella of Vapor. However, dreampunk is defined by its use of Field Recordings and strong Progressive Electronic influences, and often puts much less of a focus on sampled material. Dreampunk also exhibits a more cinematic mood, and utilizes visuals of Cyberpunk and urban environments. These characteristics set it apart from slushwave's layered production, hypnagogic atmosphere, minimalistic artwork, and firm grounding in the sound of vaporwave, including its strong dependence on samples.
